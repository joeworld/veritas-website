WHAT WE DO

THE SCALE OF THE PROBLEM

Over 180 million Nigerians are either off-grid or experience unreliable grid service. The economy is powered largely by small-scale generators (10–15 GW) which is of poor quality and emits toxic air and noise pollution. As a result, Nigerian households and businesses spend almost $14 billion (₦ 5 trillion) annually on expensive and inefficient generation at $0.40/kWh or ₦140/kWh or more. Providing access to off-grid solar home systems (SHS) alternatives to complement the unreliable grid creates a $2B/YR (₦700B/YR) market opportunity that will help Nigerian homes and small businesses save over 20% of their monthly income. This makes Nigeria the biggest and most attractive off-grid opportunity in Africa for solar home systems. Like most nascent industries, the Nigerian off-grid solar market faces diverse problems like low consumer awareness and purchasing power, shifting patterns of demand, high upfront cost of product financing, fragmented data and solutions (no dominant player), fluctuations in exchange rates, unsustainable operation costs, serious competition by mini-generators, and regulatory lags – it is a complex problem.

OUR SOLUTION

However, the off-grid solar market has huge growth potential, especially for any company that understands the intricacies of the market and country (which we do). We recognize that one individual company cannot solve this alone. To kickstart growth, there has to be a collaborative shared vision and effort to raise awareness, provide bottom-up intelligence, stimulate demand, and build the last-mile economic infrastructure needed to educate and support customers and other key stakeholders like last-mile agents, regulators, contributors, etc. Veritas Vision seeks to be the catalyst in that community. With our understanding of the market and collaborative mindset, we are building a suite of solutions needed to enable sustainable delivery of life-changing solutions targeted at the bottom billions to help them break out of the cycle of poverty.
