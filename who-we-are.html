Who We Are

Our vision

A healthy, educated, equitable, and sustainable world where the poor has access to all the resources, information, products and services they need to break out of the cycle of poverty.



Our purpose

We seek to be the catalyst in the communities of manufacturers, last-mile distributors, customers, agents, partners, regulators, institutions, and contributors in creating inclusive, sustainable, and innovative solutions to help disadvantaged Nigerians break out the cycle of poverty.

Our core values

  • We love the problems and hold our solutions lightly.
  • We believe in local problems, local solutions.
  • We dream audaciously and build lean.
  • We believe in the power of communities.
  • We round up unusual partnerships.
  • We wow our customers and partners always.
  • We believe inclusion, education and collaboration is key to the success of our industry.
  • We are frugal, not just for ourselves but for the ecosystem.

Our story

Andrew Amedu, Founder of Veritas Vision, was raised in a disadvantaged lower-class neighbourhood in Nigeria. There, he witnessed firsthand how a lack of awareness and access to information, resources and alternative solutions can lock generations of people in the cycle of poverty.

In 2016, while studying Earth Science in the Netherlands, one of Andrewâ€™s Professors introduced him to The FocusSpecs Adjustable Glasses. This opened his eyes to other sustainable and life-changing innovations that are inaccessible to poor communities. Thereafter, Andrew joined Impact Hub Amsterdam to learn more about social entrepreneurship. He instantly became fascinated by the power of social entrepreneurship and sustainable technological innovations to solve socio-economic and environmental challenges in the developing world particularly in his country, Nigeria, where over 70% of the population live in universally unacceptable conditions.

In October 2017, Andrew decided to set up Veritas Vision, a for-profit social enterprise that envisions a healthy, educated, equitable, and sustainable world where the poor have access to the resources, information, products and services they need to break out of the cycle of poverty.

In 2018, having gathered validated bottom-up data, we noticed that there was a dire problem both on the demand and supply sides of the off-grid solar industry. Being a social enterprise that is obsessed with unparalleled impact, we were also not convinced that our earlier mission of simply financing alternative life-changing solutions would significantly help the poor to break out of poverty because their problems are deep-rooted and multifaceted.

We are currently pivoting to solve the off-grid solar multi-sided problem through collaboration with key stakeholders to build the last-mile economic infrastructure needed to enable sustainable delivery of life-changing solutions targeted at the bottom billions to help them break out of the cycle of poverty.
