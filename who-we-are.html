About Verita

Who We Are.

OUR VISION.

A healthy, educated, equitable, and sustainable world where the poor has access to all the resources, information, products and services they need to break out of the cycle of poverty.

OUR PURPOSE.

We seek to be the catalyst in the communities of manufacturers, last-mile distributors, customers, agents, partners, regulators, institutions, and contributors in creating inclusive, sustainable, and innovative solutions to help disadvantaged Africans break out the cycle of poverty.

Our Story So Far

Our Story.

Andrew Amedu, Founder of Veritas Vision, was raised in a disadvantaged neighbourhood in Nigeria. There, he witnessed firsthand how a lack of access to information, resources and alternative products and services can lock generations of people in the cycle of poverty. In 2016, while studying Earth Science in the Netherlands, one of his Professors introduced him to The FocusSpecs Adjustable Glasses. This opened his eyes to other sustainable and life-changing innovations that are unaccessible to poor communities. Andrew became convinced of the power of social entrepreneurship and sustainable innovations to solve socio-economic and environmental challenges in the developing world. He decided to start in his home country Nigeria where over 70% of the population live in universally unacceptable conditions.

In October 2017, Andrew set up Veritas Vision, a for-profit social enterprise that envisions a healthy, educated, equitable, and sustainable world where the poor have access to the resources, information, products and services they need to break out of the cycle of poverty. Veritas Vision set out to combat poverty and climate change by financing life-changing solutions for low-income and BOP families in Nigeria via a distribution network of micro-social-entrepreneurs (called “Visioneers”). Our Visioneers offer our beneficiaries the choice to pay cash or via comfortable payment plans (PayGo) according to their income and provide prompt after-sales services (all of our products come with a 2-year clear and simple warranty).

We launched in peri-urban Alimosho, Lagos with 5 Visioneers and a data-driven and consultative approach to sales. We tested several target audiences with 3 different life-changing products: the focusspecs adjustable glasses, solar lanterns and home systems (GreenLightPlanet and DLight), and biomass cookstoves (Envirofit) to identify the biggest need and what customers would pay for it. In October 2018, we saw that over 75% of our sales came from solar products and decided to focus on selling only solar as that represented the biggest need of our target audience. Additionally, about 80% of our customers opted for payment plans (PAYGO).

Till date, with only 2 Visioneers on the field, we have north of 530 customers with over 600 units sold. We have gathered valuable bottom-up data through our dialogue with suppliers and customers and won their hearts with our impeccable after-sales services. Veritas Vision has tested several innovative ideas to address some barriers to scale namely salesforce, sourcing the right solar products, and low-cost financing for PayGo.

Armed with data from our customers, employee feedback, market research, industry reports, and interviews with country managers of off-grid solar manufacturers, we noticed that there was a dire problem both on the demand and supply sides. Being a social enterprise that is obsessed with unparalleled impact, we were not convinced that our earlier mission of simply financing alternative life-changing solutions was going to significantly help the poor to break out of poverty because their problems are deep-rooted and multifaceted. We are currently pivoting to solve the off-grid solar multi-sided problem through collaboration with key stakeholders to build the last-mile economic infrastructure needed to enable sustainable delivery of life-changing solutions targeted at the bottom billions to help them break out of the cycle of poverty.
