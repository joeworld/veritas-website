The Nigerian off-grid opportunity

Over 180 million Nigerians are either off-grid or experience unreliable grid service. The economy is powered largely by small-scale generators (10–15 GW) which is of poor quality and emits toxic air and noise pollution.
As a result, Nigerian households and businesses spend almost $14 billion (₦ 5 trillion) annually on expensive and inefficient generation at $0.40/kWh or ₦140/kWh or more.
Providing access to off-grid solar home systems (SHS) alternatives to complement the unreliable grid creates a $2B/YR (₦700B/YR) market opportunity that will help Nigerian homes and small businesses save over 20% of their monthly income. However, like most nascent industries, the Nigerian off-grid solar market faces diverse problems but it has huge growth potential, especially for any company that understands the intricacies of the market and country – which we do.
